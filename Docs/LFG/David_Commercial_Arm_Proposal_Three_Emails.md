## 1. Commercial arm structure and equity proposal

Max,

I've taken time to properly think through the commercial arm proposal and what we both ultimately want for OASIS long term.

First, I want to say clearly — I value what we've built together. You've invested real capital, time, and belief, and we genuinely make a strong team. As we move toward token launch and MVP releases, I agree that evolving the structure to support scale makes sense.

I'm aligned that we should establish a dedicated commercial arm.

Where I want to be thoughtful is in making sure the structure gives you real authority and visibility on the business side, while also protecting the long-term mission and vision of OASIS that I've stewarded for the past 15 years.

Here's what I propose:

**OASIS Open Source (Foundation Layer)**

- David: CEO / CTO / Founder  
- Max: COO / CMO  
- No change here — this keeps the core mission stable.

**OASIS Commercial Arm**

- David: CEO & Chairman (Technology, Architecture, Long-Term Vision)  
- Max: President / COO / CMO (Commercial Leadership & Operations)  
- Product strategy, long-term direction, and major strategic decisions would be shared between us and agreed jointly.

As President, you would:

- Lead day-to-day commercial operations  
- Drive revenue, partnerships, growth and execution  
- Represent OASIS in business meetings, negotiations, investor discussions and strategic partnerships  
- Oversee hiring, finance, marketing and operational scaling  

In practice, this gives you full authority over business execution and commercial representation, while ensuring we remain aligned on long-term strategy and mission.

To further align incentives and reflect your expanded leadership role, I'm also proposing:

- An increase from 22% to **up to 30% equity** in the commercial arm  
- The additional **8% to vest over 4 years**  
- Vesting tied to continued involvement and commercial performance milestones  
- Standard good leaver / bad leaver provisions  

This ensures:

- You are meaningfully rewarded for scaling the business  
- We are fully aligned long term  
- The structure remains stable and investor-ready  

I believe this approach gives you real authority, recognition, and upside — while keeping governance clean and avoiding role ambiguity that can create issues later.

Most importantly, it keeps us aligned as partners building something long term and sustainable.

Let's discuss this openly and refine together if needed.

## 3. Formal operating structure and governance

Max,

I've been reflecting on our conversation and on the next phase of what we're building.

First, I want to reiterate something clearly: I deeply value what you've done and sacrificed. Selling your house, investing $300k, living lean for years, and standing by the vision when others didn't — that matters to me. The three promotions and equity increase were my way of recognising that commitment and aligning us long term.

At the same time, if we're stepping into a more serious phase — President of the commercial arm, expanded equity, clearer authority — then we also need to step into a more professional and formal operating structure.

To be candid, some of the tension we've had in the past has come from communication gaps, delayed follow-ups, and decisions made without alignment. I'm not raising that to criticise you — I know we've both been under pressure — but to acknowledge that informality has created friction and eroded trust at times.

If we're going to scale properly, especially with government pilots, JV structures, and larger partners, we can't rely on informal understandings. We need clarity, written governance, and structured reporting.

So I'd like to propose that part of formalising the three-promotion structure includes:

- A proper, comprehensive **shareholder and operating agreement**  
- Clearly defined **"Joint Major Decisions"** vs President authority  
- Defined **tech stewardship and IP boundaries**  
- Agreed **equity/option pool parameters**  
- A formal **reporting cadence** (weekly exec sync, monthly strategic review, quarterly review)  
- Clear **communication and documentation standards**  

This isn't about control — it's about durability.

If we're building something meant to last decades, it has to be architected like a real institution, not managed informally.

I believe this protects both of us:

- It protects your operational authority by defining it clearly.  
- It protects my long-term stewardship by formalising it.  
- It protects the company by eliminating ambiguity.  

The promotions and equity increase stand. But they need to sit inside a properly documented structure so we both feel secure.

Let's design this the right way and have it properly drafted and signed so we remove uncertainty permanently.

I'm committed to building this with you — professionally and structurally sound.

David
