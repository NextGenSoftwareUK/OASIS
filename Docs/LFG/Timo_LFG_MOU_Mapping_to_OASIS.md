## 2.3 Equity / value-sharing structure (adapt)

**Timo:** 10% on signing + 5% performance (Annex A).

**OASIS (revised):** OASIS is already well developed, so **lower initial equity** and **more equity tied to KPIs**; **revenue share on B2G deals can be proportionally higher** (see Section 3).

**Suggested structure:**

- **Equity cap:** **10% maximum** (initial + performance combined). Keeps the cap table tight; LFG’s main upside is **revenue share** on B2G deals.
- **Initial equity:** Low — e.g. **1–2%** on signing, to reflect that OASIS is already developed and upside is in rev share.
- **Performance equity:** Pool sized so **initial + performance ≤ 10%** in all cases — e.g. **6–8%** total for Annex A KPIs (narrative, gov intros, pilots, conversion, portfolio integration, fundraising). So e.g. 2% initial + up to 8% performance = 10% max.
- **B2G revenue share:** **Primary upside** — e.g. **20–25%** (or up to 30%) of revenue from LFG-originated/facilitated government deals for first 2 years per deal (see Section 3). This is where most of LFG’s value comes from.

**Options to offer (all within 10% equity cap):**

- **Option A** — 2% initial + up to 8% performance (Annex A) + **20–25% rev share** on B2G deals (first 2 years per deal). Focus on rev share.  
- **Option B** — 0% initial + up to 10% performance + **25–30% rev share** on B2G deals. Maximum rev-share focus.  
- **Option C** — 1% initial + up to 9% performance + **20–25% rev share** on B2G deals.

**Recommendation:** Option A or B — equity capped at 10%, with **revenue share as the main lever** for LFG’s upside on closed deals.

**Equity ceiling (cap):** **Total equity issuable to LFG under this MOU shall not exceed 10% fully diluted** (initial + performance combined). LFG’s principal compensation for deal flow is **B2G revenue share**, not equity. Include this language in the MOU.

# 3. Revenue share research – What advisory/origination partners typically get

*Research on what to pay a partner who originates/facilitates deals (B2B, enterprise, and context for B2G).*

**Enterprise B2B / SaaS – partner who arranges or closes deals**

- **Closed by partner (“on partner’s paper”):** often **25–40%+** of first-year Annual Contract Value (ACV).  
- **Qualified opportunities (lead ready to buy):** often **~20%** of first-year ACV.  
- **Warm leads only:** often **~10%** of first-year ACV.  
- **Marketing-only (no sales):** **15–20%** of first-year ACV.  
- **General finder’s fee range:** **5–35%** of deal value depending on involvement; **35–40%** of first-year ACV for closed leads is common in SaaS.

**Channel / BD partner revenue share (B2B)**

- **Typical rev share range:** **20–40%** of transaction value; some programmes do **up to 50% for the first year only**.  
- Structures: flat %, tiered by volume, or performance-based (retention, upsells).

**Implications for OASIS–LFG**

- LFG is doing **origination + facilitation** (intros, narrative, pilot structuring, relationship management) and may co-lead or support closing. They are closer to “qualified opportunity” or “closed with partner” than “warm lead only.”  
- **Reasonable B2G rev share range:** **15–30%** of revenue from LFG-originated/facilitated deals — towards **20–25%** if they lead the relationship and facilitation; up to **25–30%** if they are heavily involved in closing and ongoing account support.  
- **Duration:** Often **first 1–3 years** of the contract (or of revenue from that deal). First-year-only is also common and keeps your margin higher.  
- **Definition:** Clearly define “originated/facilitated” (e.g. LFG made the intro and was materially involved in the path to signature; or LFG listed in the contract as channel/partner).

**Caveat – US federal government**

- In **US federal** procurement, “contingent fees” for soliciting or obtaining government contracts are heavily restricted (bona fide employees or established agencies only).  
- Many of LFG’s engagements are **non-US** (e.g. Africa, GCC, EU). For those, commercial rev-share benchmarks above apply. For any US federal deal, structure and legal advice should be sought so any partner compensation complies with local rules.

**Suggested OASIS range**

- **20–25%** of revenue from each LFG-originated/facilitated B2G deal, for the **first 2 years** of that deal (or first 2 years of revenue), with “originated/facilitated” defined in the MOU.  
- Option: **tiered** — e.g. 25% year 1, 15% year 2, 10% year 3 — to align LFG with deal success while reducing long-term cost.

### 3.1 Token allocation if LFG assists with TGE

*Benchmarks for paying a partner who helps design and execute a token launch.*

**Advisor allocations (typical)**

- Advisors in crypto projects typically receive **1–3%** of total token supply.  
- Examples: Blur Foundation allocates **1%** to advisors with 4–5 year vesting and 4–16 month cliff.  
- 2025 tokenomics benchmarks: advisor/partner allocations **1–3%**; team 18–20%; investors 12–18%.

**Launch / execution partner allocations**

- Partners who do more than advice (tokenomics design, TGE execution, narrative, exchange strategy, investor targeting) often sit between “advisor” and “launchpad”:  
  - **Building / launch partners:** e.g. 3–8% (IDOS 7.6% to building partners; SubQuery 15% team + launch partners combined; Syndicate 3.25% pre-launch partnerships with milestone vesting).  
  - **Marketing / incentive pools:** often 2–8% with vesting.

**Vesting norms**

- **Advisors:** often **4–5 year** vesting, **4–16 month cliff**, then linear.  
- **Launch partners:** often **6–12 month cliff** + **24 month** linear vesting, or 24-month lock + 24-month vest.

**Suggested range for LFG (TGE support)**

- **2–5%** of total token supply if LFG provides material TGE support (tokenomics design, launch execution, narrative, exchange/market-maker coordination, investor targeting).  
  - **2–3%** if their role is advisory + limited execution.  
  - **3–5%** if they lead or heavily execute the TGE (e.g. run the process, key exchange intros, investor roadshow).  
- **Vesting:** e.g. **12 month cliff** + **24 month linear** vesting (or 24+24) so alignment is long-term and unlock doesn’t dump at launch.  
- **Condition:** Allocation only if/when OASIS proceeds with a TGE and LFG’s support is contractually agreed (per LFG proposal: Scenarios 1 or 2 — token deferred or decoupled from core infra).
