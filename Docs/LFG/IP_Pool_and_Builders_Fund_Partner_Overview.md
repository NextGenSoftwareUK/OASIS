# IP Pool & Builders Fund — Partner Overview

We are proposing an **IP Pool** — a neutral, not-for-profit commons that holds IP, pays creators back, and licenses to the world — and a **Builders Fund**, through which teams use that IP, build companies with tokenised agents and equity, and contribute new IP back to the pool (with bursaries and funding for builders). This document explains the concept, the partners, and how it all fits together, so that any partner can understand the offer and decide how to engage.

---

## 1. How the IP Pool works (general)

The **IP Pool** is a shared commons: a single, neutral, not-for-profit structure that holds intellectual property contributed by partners, values it, pays the original creators back over time (tithing), and licenses that IP to the world under clear terms. It is proposed to house **core technology and creative IP** from the founding partners — including **OASIS** (holonic architecture, data layer, and related technology), **Hitchhiker’s Guide to the Galaxy** (brand, narrative, guides and guidance, **characters that can be used as agents** — e.g. Marvin, Deep Thought — and associated creative IP), and **Pangea / Launchboard** (tokenised cap table and **agentic equity** IP — entity formation, agent-first equity, equity minted against the entity) — and to **accept new contributions** from builders and partners (agents, ventures, modules, data) on agreed terms, with tithing back to those contributors. No one company owns the pool; the commons does, and it grows as more IP is contributed.

Operationally, the pool:

- **Holds IP** — Contributed by participating organisations (technology, code, brands, creative IP) under clear legal contract. The pool becomes the single, trusted **licensing body** for that IP.
- **Values and pays back creators** — IP is valued at fair value. A **capped** return (e.g. a share of licensing and commercial revenue) flows back to the people and organisations who created or contributed it — **tithing** — until that value is repaid. After that, the IP stays in the commons; optional ongoing tithing can fund infrastructure and research.
- **Licenses to the world** — Governments, enterprises, and builders **license** from the pool under defined terms. They don’t buy the IP; they use it under clear, machine- and human-readable terms. Commercial ventures (companies, JVs, products) can be fully commercial; the pool just defines the licensing rules.
- **Accepts new contributions** — What builders and partners create (new agents, ventures, modules, data) can be **contributed or licensed into the pool** on agreed terms, with tithing back to the contributors. So the pool **grows** over time and stays aligned with creators.

**Why it matters for any partner:** You get a **single place** where IP is held, valued, and licensed. No one company “owns” the story; the commons does. Creators get paid back; licensees get clarity; governments and enterprises get a structure they can trust for critical infrastructure and creative economy.

## 2. Comparison with Story Protocol

[Story Protocol](https://www.story.foundation/) (Story Foundation) is **AI-native infrastructure for IP**: it tokenises IP and makes it **programmable** (ownership → remix → monetisation), with programmable licensing and onchain enforcement (attribution and royalties across derivative graphs). Story’s goal is to unlock the ~$80T IP asset class for AI and creators.

**How we align with Story:**

- **Programmable IP / licensing** — We also want IP with **clear, enforceable terms** that can support automated flows. Our primary mechanism is a **commons** (stewardship, contracts, tithing) with optional **onchain tithing** for transparency. Story is onchain-first; we are commons-first with optional onchain.
- **Creator payback** — Story: attribution and royalties on derivative graphs. Us: **tithing** — a share of licensing and commercial revenue flows back to creators until a cap, then the IP remains in the commons. Same idea: **creators get paid from use and derivatives**.
- **Tokenisation** — We tokenise **assets and rights** (e.g. cap tables, equity, NFTs) and can represent IP and payback in the pool in a way that could **integrate with Story** (e.g. commons holds the IP; Story could be the onchain layer for programmable licensing and royalties).
- **AI + IP** — Story: “AI runs on IP”; rights-cleared data for AI. We: **IP in the pool is usable by both humans and agents** — clearly licensed, structured (e.g. holonic data), so AI agents can discover, use, and reason over it (see §3). Same theme: **IP that works for humans and for AI**.

We use [Story](https://www.story.foundation/) as a **reference** for positioning and for branding (see §7).

## 3. Making IP usable to both humans and agents

The IP in the pool is designed to be **usable by humans and by AI agents**:

- **Humans** — Clear licensing terms, contracts, and narrative. Governments, enterprises, and builders can license from the pool, build products, form companies, and contribute back. Tithing is contractually defined (and optionally onchain for transparency).
- **Agents** — The **data and structure** of the IP (e.g. holonic architecture: identity-independent, multi-provider, whole/part) are **machine-readable**. Agents can:
  - **Discover** what’s in the pool (e.g. by capability, by parent, by licence type).
  - **Use** licensed IP under defined terms (reasoning, generation, tool use).
  - **Contribute** new IP (e.g. reasoning graphs, trained models, generated assets) that can be valued and added to the pool with tithing back.

So the pool isn’t only a legal wrapper — it’s a **licensing and data layer** that both humans and agents can interact with. That’s why we integrate with **OpenSERV’s BRAID reasoning** and **SERV agents** (§6): agents that reason over shared graphs and register as services can use and extend the IP the pool holds.

## 5. How it works with the Builders Fund

When **OpenSERV** launches the Builders Fund, it can **leverage IP from the IP Pool**, allow new builders to **contribute their IP to the pool**, and benefit from tooling designed for **end-to-end agentic company formation** that is fully on-chain and interoperable. The IP Pool and the Builders Fund work together and strengthen each other — while also helping builders.

**What the Builders Fund offers:**

1. **Use the IP** — Teams **license from the pool** and build with the open-core stack (holonic architecture, OpenSERV agent stack, Hitchhikers theme as overlay). They form companies, ship products, run ventures.
2. **Spin up companies with cap tables** — Via **Pangea** (Launchboard, agent-first equity): entity formation, cap table, share classes, stakeholders, document signing. Builders get **real companies**, not only demos.
3. **Tokenise their agents** — Agent-first design: one **entity holon** per org, **lead agent**, **swarm** of agents. Equity is minted **against** the entity (metadata: entity, lead agent). So agents sit inside a **legal entity with a cap table** and **tokenised equity**.
4. **Generate equity that gets tokenised** — Cap table defines who owns what; **equity tokens** (e.g. Web4/Web3 via OASIS) are minted for share classes. Revenue flows to entity treasury; **distribution to equity holders** per cap table. So builders **monetise** and **get tokenised equity** in their own ventures.
5. **Contribute new IP to the pool** — What builders create (agents, ventures, modules, data) can be **contributed or licensed into the IP Pool** on agreed terms (fair value, capped tithing back to the team). The pool grows; creators get paid back.

**Bursaries and funding:**

- **Bursaries to top teams** — The Builders Fund offers **bursaries** (non-repayable grants or stipends) to **top teams** that use the IP, build with the stack, and (where applicable) contribute IP to the pool. Criteria: quality of build, use of open-core stack, alignment with pool and fund goals.
- **Funding to teams building with OpenSERV** — **OpenSERV** provides **funding** to teams that build using the OpenSERV stack (agents, BRAID, SERV, launchpad). So builders get: (i) **use of the IP** from the pool, (ii) **Pangea** for companies and tokenised equity, (iii) **OpenSERV** funding and support when they build with OpenSERV, and (iv) **bursaries** from the Builders Fund for top performers.

That’s how the IP Pool and the Builders Fund work together: **pool = where IP lives and is licensed**; **fund = where builders use it, build companies, tokenise agents and equity, and contribute new IP back**, with **bursaries** and **OpenSERV funding** to make it viable.

## 6. Funding and co-marketing through the shared vehicle

Partners benefit from **seeking funding together** and from **co-marketing** under one narrative and one brand.

**Funding through the shared vehicle**

We will seek funding (e.g. from governments, foundations, impact investors, or aligned VCs) **through this shared structure** — the IP Pool and the Builders Fund — rather than each partner raising alone. One vehicle, one story: a neutral commons that holds IP, pays creators back, and licenses to the world; plus a Builders Fund that lets teams use that IP, form companies, tokenise agents and equity, and contribute new IP back, with bursaries and OpenSERV funding. Funders get a **single diligence surface** (pool + fund, partners, governance) and a **clear use of funds** (pool operations, tithing, bursaries, builder support). Partners can **co-present** to funders, share pipeline, and allocate raised capital to pool, fund, and partner priorities by agreement. So: **raise together, deploy through the same vehicle**, and grow the commons and the builder ecosystem in lockstep.

**Co-marketing**

Partners benefit from **aligned messaging and joint visibility**. When we promote the IP Pool or the Builders Fund (website, events, social, press), **all core partners** are part of the story: Hitchhikers (theme, characters-as-agents), OASIS (holonic stack), Pangea (companies, tokenised equity), OpenSERV (launchpad, BRAID, SERV, funding), Wolfgang (brand, website). The **Wolfgang** brand and website (§8) give us one place to land and one narrative; campaigns, launches, and builder outreach can be **co-branded**. Events (e.g. 42 locations, demo days, Burning Man) become **shared activation** — each partner's audience sees the full offer. So: **one brand, one story, shared amplification**; no one partner has to carry the whole message alone.

## 7. OpenSERV: launchpad, BRAID reasoning, SERV agents

We use OpenSERV in three main ways:

**OpenSERV launchpad**  
OpenSERV’s **tokenisation launchpad** lets projects and ventures **launch tokens** (e.g. for community, utility, or ecosystem). When builders in the Builders Fund form companies and want to tokenise (e.g. project token, community token), we use **OpenSERV’s launchpad** where it fits: same ecosystem, aligned narrative, and support for teams building on OpenSERV. So: **entity + cap table + equity tokens** (via Pangea/OASIS) and **project/community token** (via OpenSERV launchpad) when applicable.

**BRAID reasoning**  
**BRAID** is OpenSERV’s **bounded reasoning** technology (e.g. up to ~74× efficiency gains in inference). We integrate it with the **holonic architecture** (OASIS) as **Holonic BRAID**: **shared reasoning graphs** stored as **holons**, so many agents reuse the same graph — persistent, provider-agnostic. That makes IP and reasoning **usable by agents** at scale and keeps it in a form that can live in or be licensed from the pool. So: **BRAID** = how agents reason; **holons** = where that reasoning is stored and shared; **pool** = where the resulting IP and graphs can be held and licensed.

**SERV agents**  
**SERV** is the infrastructure for **agent registration and discovery** (e.g. ONET, A2A). Agents register as **services** with capabilities; other agents and humans discover them by capability or by context (e.g. “agents under this entity”). We use **SERV agents** so that:

- Builders’ **agents** (lead agent, swarm) can be **registered and discovered** (e.g. by entity, by capability).
- Agents can **use the IP** from the pool (licensed, structured) and **contribute** new IP (e.g. reasoning outputs, graphs) that can be valued and added to the pool.
- The Builders Fund is **agent-native**: build with the stack, register agents via SERV, tokenise equity via Pangea/OASIS, get funding via OpenSERV.

So: **Launchpad** = tokenisation for projects/ventures; **BRAID** = efficient, shared reasoning (Holonic BRAID with the pool); **SERV agents** = discoverable agent layer that uses and contributes to the pool.
