## Proposed model: OASIS Commercial

OASIS (the open source project) remains the technology, community, and IP asset under its current leadership. **OASIS Commercial** is a separate commercial entity that licenses OASIS technology and brand. Under this proposal, **OASIS Commercial** is the counterparty to LFG/NORM: it signs the MOU, issues equity and grants revenue share to LFG, owns and operates B2G deals and pilots, and leads go-to-market and fundraising. This keeps the open source project independent and gives LFG a single, clear commercial partner for all terms.

---

## Commercial structure

| Element | Proposal |
|--------|----------|
| **Equity cap** | 10% total (initial + performance) in OASIS Commercial |
| **Initial equity** | 1–2% on signing |
| **Performance equity** | Up to 6–8% vesting on Annex A KPIs |
| **LFG’s main upside** | B2G revenue share (not equity) |
| **B2G revenue share** | 20–25% of revenue from LFG-originated/facilitated deals, first 2 years per deal |
| **Fundraising** | No cash success fees; compensation via equity + performance + revenue share |

**Options:** A) 2% initial + 8% performance + 20–25% rev share · B) 0% initial + 10% performance + 25–30% rev share · C) 1% initial + 9% performance + 20–25% rev share

## B2G revenue share

| Item | Proposal |
|------|----------|
| **Percentage** | 20–25% of revenue from each LFG-originated/facilitated B2G deal (up to 25–30% if LFG heavily involved in closing and support) |
| **Duration** | First 2 years of that deal (or first 2 years of revenue from that deal) |
| **Definition** | “Originated/facilitated” to be defined in MOU (e.g. LFG made intro + materially involved in path to signature) |
| **Optional tiered** | e.g. 25% year 1, 15% year 2, 10% year 3 |
| **Scope** | Applies to government or qualifying enterprise deals; US federal may have contingent-fee restrictions (legal advice per jurisdiction) |

## Alignment with LFG’s proposal (what we’re responding to)

- **Token strategy:** We align with resolving token strategy as a gating item. Our TGE terms above assume Scenarios 1 or 2 (defer or decouple from core infra). We will share OpenServe launchpad terms as part of next steps.
- **Cost allocation (per LFG):** LFG covers gov relationship maintenance, event costs, PR/narrative execution, deal structuring. OASIS Commercial covers tech development, platform hosting/API, security audits, local entity setup in deployment countries.
- **Portfolio integration:** Where OASIS integrates into existing LFG deployments (Pose/Pebble, ZK Pass, etc.), commercial terms follow the underlying government contract, with OASIS Commercial as technology vendor.
- **Joint ventures:** In jurisdictions requiring local entities for government contracting, a JV structure may be established; terms per country.
- **Technical collaboration:** ZK compatibility assessment (ORM/data APIs + ZK proof frameworks) and third-party security audit scoping remain part of the joint work; OASIS Commercial covers audit costs per cost allocation above.
- **B2G use cases:** Partnership supports the four B2G value propositions LFG identified: government data interop/migration (incl. ZK), geospatial credentialing/workforce verification (e.g. Pose/Pebble), programmable benefits/incentives, AI data infrastructure (Holonic Braid).
