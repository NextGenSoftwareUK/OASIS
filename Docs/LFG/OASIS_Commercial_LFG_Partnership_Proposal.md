# 1. Purpose and structure (OASIS Commercial as vehicle)

This proposal outlines the scope and conditions for a strategic partnership between **LFG/NORM** and **OASIS**, using **OASIS Commercial** as the commercial vehicle for the engagement.

**Why OASIS Commercial**

- **OASIS** (the open source project) remains the technology and community asset; its leadership and IP stay with the existing OASIS structure.
- **OASIS Commercial** is a separate commercial entity that licenses OASIS technology and brand. It is the entity that will:
  - Sign the MOU and any definitive agreements with LFG/NORM.
  - Issue equity and grant revenue share to LFG.
  - Own and operate B2G deals, pilots, fundraising, and go-to-market.
  - Report to LFG and coordinate narrative, pipeline, and governance.
- This separation keeps the open source project independent while giving LFG a clear, single counterparty (OASIS Commercial) for all commercial terms, equity, and revenue share.

**Purpose of the partnership**

- **LFG/NORM** provides narrative architecture (gov-facing), B2G market entry, pilot facilitation, portfolio integration (e.g. Pose/Pebble, ZK Pass), token/fundraising advisory, and technical collaboration (ZK, security, hardware).
- **OASIS Commercial** provides the technology (licensed from OASIS), execution, and commercial structure; retains product ownership and tech delivery.
- Compensation to LFG is a combination of **equity in OASIS Commercial** (capped) and **revenue share on B2G deals** (primary upside).

# 4. Commercial structure (equity, revenue share, cap)

**Equity in OASIS Commercial**

- **Cap:** Total equity issuable to LFG under this MOU shall not exceed **10% fully diluted** (initial + performance combined). LFG’s principal compensation for deal flow is **B2G revenue share**, not equity.
- **Initial equity:** **1–2%** on signing of the MOU (or definitive agreement), to reflect that OASIS technology is already developed and upside is weighted to revenue share.
- **Performance equity:** Up to **6–8%** in total, vesting on achievement of Annex A KPIs (narrative locked, gov intros, pilots, pilot→production, portfolio integration, Holonic Braid pilot, institutional round). Example: 2% initial + up to 8% performance = 10% max.
- **Clawback:** Unvested performance equity may be reallocated if KPIs are not met within agreed timelines, subject to mutual review and good-faith renegotiation.

**Options (all within 10% equity cap)**

- **Option A:** 2% initial + up to 8% performance (Annex A) + **20–25% revenue share** on B2G deals (first 2 years per deal).
- **Option B:** 0% initial + up to 10% performance + **25–30% revenue share** on B2G deals.
- **Option C:** 1% initial + up to 9% performance + **20–25% revenue share** on B2G deals.

**B2G revenue share**

- **20–25%** of revenue from each LFG-originated/facilitated B2G deal, for the **first 2 years** of that deal (or first 2 years of revenue), with “originated/facilitated” defined in the MOU.
- Optional: tiered (e.g. 25% year 1, 15% year 2, 10% year 3).
- See Section 9 for benchmarks and US federal caveat.

**No fundraising fees**

- LFG does not receive cash commission or success fees on capital raised; compensation is via equity, performance equity, and revenue share.

# 7. Narrative – coordination with Wolfgang Project

OASIS already works with **Wolfgang Project** on core narrative (Holon-centred story, creative strategy). To avoid duplication and clarify LFG’s compensation:

- **Wolfgang Project:** Primary partner for core narrative — anchor story, Holon concept, elevator pitch (30s / 2m / 10m), brand and creative direction.
- **LFG/NORM:** Complementary role: (i) **Government- and institutional-facing** narrative and materials (decks, one-pagers, ministerial/investor language), (ii) **Alignment and sign-off** so the same story works for B2G and for LFG’s introductions, (iii) Optional joint workshop with Wolfgang to lock one coherent narrative.
- **KPI “narrative locked”:** Defined as core narrative (Wolfgang-led) + gov-facing/institutional version (LFG-led or LFG sign-off) agreed and signed off by OASIS Commercial. LFG’s performance equity for this KPI reflects their part (gov-facing, alignment, validation).
- **Annex B vendors:** If a narrative/PR vendor is engaged, the parties will specify whether it is Wolfgang, an LFG-nominated vendor, or a shared choice so budgets and scope are clear.

**Proposed wording for the MOU:**  
*“OASIS Commercial is already working with Wolfgang Project on the core narrative and Holon story. LFG/NORM will own the government- and institutional-facing version and align with Wolfgang so there is one coherent story. The ‘narrative locked’ KPI is the joint deliverable: core narrative + gov-facing materials agreed.”*

# 8. Governance, confidentiality, term and termination

**Governance**

- LFG receives one (1) voting board seat (or observer, if preferred) on OASIS Commercial.
- Quarterly meetings on KPI progress, B2G pipeline, fundraising, and narrative.
- Operational and financial updates as reasonably requested by LFG, subject to confidentiality.

**Confidentiality**

- All non-public information shared under the MOU remains confidential for three (3) years post-termination.

**Term**

- Initial term of 24 months from the Effective Date, extendable by agreement. Either party may terminate with 30 days’ written notice.

**Termination**

- Vested equity and any earned revenue share remain with LFG. Unvested performance equity reverts (or is reallocated) per Annex A.

**Other**

- The parties act as independent contractors; no joint venture, partnership, or employment relationship.
- Governing law: jurisdiction of OASIS Commercial (e.g. England & Wales).
- Notices by email with written confirmation. Entire agreement with binding sections (equity, revenue share, Annex B, board, confidentiality, termination/vesting) to be set out in the definitive MOU.

# 9. Revenue share and TGE token allocation (benchmarks)

**B2G revenue share – benchmarks**

- Partners who originate and facilitate enterprise/B2G deals commonly receive **15–30%** of revenue (or first-year ACV). For origination + facilitation, **20–25%** is typical; up to **25–30%** if heavily involved in closing and ongoing support.
- **Suggested for LFG:** 20–25% of revenue from each LFG-originated/facilitated B2G deal for the first 2 years of that deal, with “originated/facilitated” defined in the MOU.
- **US federal:** Contingent fees for soliciting US federal government contracts are restricted. For non-US engagements (e.g. Africa, GCC, EU), the above benchmarks apply; for any US federal deal, structure and legal advice should be sought.

**TGE token allocation (if LFG assists with a token launch)**

- If OASIS proceeds with a TGE and LFG provides material support (tokenomics design, launch execution, narrative, exchange/market-maker coordination, investor targeting), a **token allocation** is proposed:
  - **2–5%** of total token supply (2–3% advisory + limited execution; 3–5% if LFG leads or heavily executes the TGE).
  - **Vesting:** e.g. 12 month cliff + 24 month linear vesting.
  - **Condition:** Only if/when a TGE is agreed and LFG’s role is contractually defined (consistent with LFG’s Scenarios 1 or 2 — token deferred or decoupled from core infrastructure).
