# OASIS Commercial Structure Proposal

**Draft for Discussion**  
**Date:** February 2025

---

## 1. Purpose

This document proposes a formal structure that allows OASIS to grow commercially while preserving its open source identity and David's leadership of the core project. The goal is clarity: defined roles, defined entities, and the freedom for both parties to operate effectively.

---

## 2. Proposed Structure: Two Entities

### Entity A: OASIS (Existing)
- **What it is:** The open source project, core technology, and community
- **Leadership:** David remains **CEO of OASIS**
- **Responsibilities:** Product vision, architecture, technical direction, open source stewardship
- **Ownership:** David retains ownership of OASIS technology and brand

### Entity B: OASIS Commercial (New)
- **What it is:** A separate commercial company that licenses OASIS technology
- **Leadership:** Max serves as **CEO of OASIS Commercial**
- **Responsibilities:** Business development, sales, partnerships, fundraising, go-to-market
- **Relationship:** Licenses OASIS technology and brand from Entity A under agreed terms

**Key point:** David is CEO of OASIS. Max is CEO of the commercial entity. These are complementary roles, not competing ones.

---

## 3. Why This Makes Sense

### For OASIS
- The open source project stays independent and community-focused
- David leads what he built and cares about most
- No dilution of the core mission or technology

### For Growth
- Commercial activity requires a dedicated entity: sales, contracts, fundraising, hiring
- Investors and enterprise customers expect a clear corporate structure
- A commercial arm can fund and support the open source project (via royalties, grants, or contributions)

### For Both Parties
- **Clarity:** No ambiguity about who runs what
- **Freedom:** Each party operates in their domain without stepping on the other
- **Alignment:** Success of the commercial entity benefits OASIS (revenue share, visibility, resources)

---

## 4. Precedent: How Others Have Done This

This structure is standard in open source. Many of the most successful projects use it:

| Open Source Project | Commercial Entity | Structure |
|---------------------|-------------------|-----------|
| **WordPress** | Automattic | Matt Mullenweg leads both, but Automattic is the commercial company; WordPress.org is the open source project |
| **Linux** | Red Hat, SUSE, Canonical | Linus leads Linux; separate companies build commercial products on top |
| **Elasticsearch** | Elastic | Open source core; commercial company offers managed services, support, enterprise features |
| **MongoDB** | MongoDB Inc. | Open source database; MongoDB Inc. is the commercial company (CEO runs the business) |
| **GitLab** | GitLab Inc. | Open source project; GitLab Inc. is the commercial entity |
| **HashiCorp** | HashiCorp Inc. | Open source tools (Terraform, Vault); HashiCorp Inc. runs the business |

**Common pattern:** The creator/technical founder leads the open source project. A commercial entity—sometimes the same people, sometimes with a business-focused CEO—handles sales, support, and enterprise offerings. The two can coexist without conflict.

## 5. Proposed Terms (Summary)

### Roles
- **David:** CEO of OASIS. Owns and leads the open source project.
- **Max:** CEO of OASIS Commercial. Runs the commercial entity.

### License
- OASIS Commercial receives a license to use OASIS technology and brand for commercial purposes
- License terms to be defined (exclusive vs non-exclusive, territory, duration)
- OASIS retains ownership of all IP

### Revenue
- Revenue split to be agreed (e.g., 50/50 equity in OASIS Commercial, or royalty to OASIS, or hybrid)
- Ensures OASIS and David benefit from commercial success

### Governance
- David has no operational control over OASIS Commercial; Max runs it day-to-day
- OASIS Commercial does not control OASIS; David runs the open source project
- Optional: board seat, advisory role, or veto on major decisions (to be negotiated)

### Brand
- OASIS Commercial may use OASIS brand under agreed guidelines (e.g., "Powered by OASIS," "OASIS Commercial")
- Protects OASIS reputation while allowing commercial use
