# The IP Commons — Protecting the Vision, Enabling Global Impact

**A proposal for stewarding OASIS in a shared structure (an IP pool or commons) that pays us, protects the vision, and gives us the freedom to expand through commercial ventures using the core IP.**

**Prepared for:** David Ellams (Founder, CEO & CTO — Next Gen World Limited / OASIS)  
**Context:** Aligning OASIS with partners who are ready to take the technology to governments, universities, and the creative economy — **OpenSERV**, **Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy**, and **LFG** — through a structure that rewards you properly and secures the legacy of what you've built.

---

## 1. Why this, and why now

You've built something that is being taken seriously at the highest level. **LFG** (Laissez-Faire Group / NORM), who work with 55+ governments and have facilitated hundreds of millions in capital, have reviewed OASIS in depth. Their assessment: the Holonic architecture, Hyperdrive, and ORM are **genuinely innovative** and directly relevant to government data infrastructure, AI efficiency, and sovereign data migration. They want to work with OASIS — but they need a structure they can take to governments and institutions without legal or narrative risk.

At the same time, **Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy** — a globally recognised brand and community built around creativity, governance, and mutual ownership — is aligning around a similar idea: put valuable IP into a **commons** that pays back the creators fairly, then steward it for the long term. **OpenSERV**, our partners in reasoning and AI efficiency (and the Braid integration), are exploring how to go all-in with OASIS and Hitchhiker's, including a potential Builders Fund and shared technical roadmap.

The moment is real: **sci-fi has landed**. The strongest way to get OASIS out to every government and institution that needs it — and to get the recognition and impact this architecture deserves — is to place the IP in a **trusted, not-for-profit commons** that pays you and the team back properly, then opens the door to licensing, partnerships, and scale. This document sets out how that works and why it is the best path for the vision, for you, and for everyone building on OASIS.

We've often said that we can't do this alone. This is the opportunity to change that: to get into a **group of other cutting-edge technologies** — OpenSERV, Hitchhiker's, LFG, and the wider ecosystem — where **the sum is stronger than the parts**. Each brings something the others don't; together we get legal and structural expertise, narrative and brand, government access, and technical depth that no single party could assemble on their own.

---

## 2. What this shared structure is

The **IP pool** (or “commons”) is a **neutral, not-for-profit holding structure** that:

- **Holds the OASIS IP** (Holonic architecture, code, brand, and related assets) under clear legal contract, so that governments and enterprises can trust it for critical infrastructure.
- **Values the IP commercially** — as if we were to sell it at fair value — and then uses a **tithing** model: a share of licensing and commercial use flows back to you (and to others who have invested in creating and incubating OASIS) until that value is fully paid back. After that, the IP remains in the commons; optional ongoing tithing can fund infrastructure and research.
- **Keeps you in charge of OASIS day to day:** **Next Gen World Limited** stays exactly what it is. You remain **Founder, CEO and CTO**. The company simply **licenses** the IP from the pool instead of holding it directly. In the short term, nothing changes in how we operate — except that we now have a structure that governments, LFG, OpenSERV, and Hitchhiker's can all point to and rely on.
- **Creates an OASIS Foundation (or equivalent)** — a research and stewardship body that can fund **your deep work** on the protocols, pay you a **salary or fellowship for life**, and put you on stage: Burning Man, government summits, press, and the story of Ready Player One and the future we're building. You are the **founder and the face** of this; the structure exists to protect and amplify that.

So: the IP moves into a **pool** that pays you back and protects the vision. Next Gen World keeps building and shipping. You keep every title and role you care about. And the world gets a clear, trustworthy story: *this is a commons; the creators are being paid back; you can build your critical infrastructure on it.*

We've said for a long time how important it is to get all the IP properly protected — but doing that properly usually means legal and structural work that can cost **millions**. Doing it this way, we can get a structure that would normally cost millions **for free, or at least at no cost to us initially**: by building it as part of a shared commons with partners who have access to legal expertise, pro bono or staged arrangements, and a trusted steering board. The same rigour and protection, without the bill landing on us first.

## 3. Financial benefits — how you get paid and stay secure

- **Capped return, not a giveaway.** We agree a **fair valuation** of the OASIS IP and a **capped** return (e.g. a percentage of licensing and commercial revenue) that flows to you until that value is repaid. This is not “we give the IP away for free.” It is “we get paid properly for the real value we created,” in a way that is transparent and contractually robust (including, if we choose, blockchain-based tithing so the terms are clear and automatic).
- **Salary / fellowship for life.** On top of the tithing return, the foundation structure can fund you to do **deep research** into the protocols — the work only you can do. You are not dependent on one company’s P&L; you are funded by the board and the commons to keep advancing the core technology.
- **The upside when governments and industry adopt OASIS.** When governments and private industry start licensing and deploying OASIS at scale, the tithing stream grows. You participate in that growth up to the agreed cap. The same structure can feed into a **new commercial company** you and the team build together — “the next SpaceX” — so the IP value you created keeps working for you.
- **Repayment for everyone who invested.** Max and anyone else who has put money and years into incubating OASIS gets their return on the same principle: fair value, paid back through the tithing mechanism. So the people who believed early are made whole, and the vision is protected for the long term.

## 6. Freedom to expand — commercial ventures using the core IP

Once the IP is in the pool, **Next Gen World Limited** is one of many possible actors that license and build on OASIS. We are not giving away the right to do commercial work — we are **unlocking it**.

- **You and the team** can create **new companies** (spinoffs, JVs, products) that license OASIS from the pool. Those ventures can be fully commercial: private IP, revenue, fundraising. The pool simply defines the licensing terms (code, brand, etc.) so that everyone — including governments and LFG’s portfolio companies — knows the rules.
- **Existing projects** that are already building on OASIS can be formalised as **ecosystem members** that license the core IP. That gives them clarity and gives us a growing ecosystem of use cases and revenue that flows back through tithing.
- **LFG and others** can do deals in specific jurisdictions (e.g. Ghana, UAE) — JVs, pilots, government contracts — using the same licensing framework. We can be one of the commercial partners in those deals, or we can focus on the core platform and let others run deployment. The point is: **from the moment the IP is in the pool, there can be many companies and many ventures** all using OASIS, and you and the team participate through tithing and through whatever commercial vehicles you choose to run.

So the commons **protects the vision** and **pays us** — and at the same time it **gives us the freedom** to expand through commercial ventures that use the core IP, without being locked into a single company or a single deal.

## 7. What stays the same, and what opens up

**What stays the same:**

- **Next Gen World Limited** remains the operating company. You remain **Founder, CEO and CTO**. Day to day, we keep building and shipping OASIS. The only change is that the company **licenses** the IP from the pool instead of holding it directly — and that change is what unlocks everything below.
- **Your role and your titles** are unchanged. You are the architect, the leader, and the face of OASIS. The foundation and the commons exist to **support** that, not to replace it.

**What opens up:**

- **Governments and universities** can adopt OASIS with a clear answer to “who owns this, and can we trust it?” — a not-for-profit commons, with creators being paid back and clear licensing terms.
- **OpenSERV, Hitchhiker's, and LFG** can commit to a shared plan: the commons, Builders Fund, narrative, and go-to-market. We get a **decision point** instead of drift.
- **Fundraising** becomes possible for the commons (e.g. family offices, impact capital) and for commercial spinoffs, with a clean separation between “commons” and “commercial” so that investors and partners know what they’re backing.
- **You** get a **guaranteed repayment** for the value you created, a **salary or fellowship** to do deep research, and the **freedom** to start or join commercial ventures that use OASIS — while the vision is protected in the commons for the long term.
