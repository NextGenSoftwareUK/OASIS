# IP Commons — Shared Plan (Descriptive)

**Purpose:** Factual description of the shared plan for an IP pool/commons. For alignment, sign-on, and collective fundraising.  
**Participants (invited to sign):** OASIS/Next Gen World Limited, Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy, OpenSERV, LFG/NORM (and other parties as agreed).  
**Status:** Draft for discussion and sign-on.  
**Context:** OASIS has been invited to pool IP with Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy for protection; OpenSERV and LFG are part of the intended shared structure. Good alignment from group discussion.

---

## 1. What the structure is

- **IP pool (commons):** A neutral, not-for-profit holding structure.
- **Function:** Holds IP (e.g. OASIS Holonic architecture, code, brand; Hitchhiker's IP; as agreed) under clear legal contract. Enables governments and enterprises to engage with a single, trusted licensing body.
- **OASIS in the pool:** OASIS IP (Holonic architecture, Hyperdrive, ORM, related assets) is contributed or assigned to the pool. Next Gen World Limited continues to operate OASIS and **licenses** the IP from the pool rather than holding it directly.
- **Governance:** A steering board and (as needed) an OASIS Foundation or equivalent for research and stewardship. Details to be set by legal/stewardship partners.

---

## 2. How creators and contributors get paid

- **Valuation and cap:** The IP is valued (fair value). A **capped** return is agreed (e.g. a percentage of licensing and commercial revenue).
- **Tithing:** A share of licensing and commercial use flows back to creators/contributors until the agreed value is repaid. After that, the IP remains in the commons; optional ongoing tithing can fund infrastructure and research.
- **Mechanics:** Terms are contractually defined. Optionally, blockchain-based tithing can be used for transparency and automation.
- **Scope of payback:** Applies to David (and/or Next Gen World), Max, and others who have invested in creating/incubating OASIS, on the same principle: fair value, repaid through the tithing mechanism. Equivalent arrangements for other contributing parties (e.g. Hitchhiker's, OpenSERV) as agreed.
- **Salary/fellowship:** The foundation/stewardship structure can fund deep research (e.g. protocols) via salary or fellowship, separate from tithing.

---

## 3. Roles and what stays the same

- **Next Gen World Limited:** Remains the operating company. David remains Founder, CEO and CTO. Day-to-day building and shipping of OASIS is unchanged. The company licenses the IP from the pool instead of holding it directly.
- **Titles and roles:** No change to David’s role as architect, leader, and public face of OASIS. The foundation/commons supports that role.

---

## 4. Commercial use of the IP

- **Licensing from the pool:** Next Gen World and other parties (new companies, JVs, ecosystem members) license the core IP from the pool under defined terms.
- **Commercial ventures:** New companies, spinoffs, JVs, and products that license from the pool can be fully commercial (private IP, revenue, fundraising). The pool defines licensing terms (code, brand, etc.).
- **Ecosystem:** Existing projects building on OASIS can be formalised as ecosystem members that license the core IP.
- **LFG and others:** Deals in specific jurisdictions (e.g. Ghana, UAE) — JVs, pilots, government contracts — use the same licensing framework. Multiple commercial partners can operate under it.

## 6. Builders Fund — use the IP, contribute new IP to the pool

- **Purpose:** The Builders Fund is the initiative through which teams **use the IP** (license from the pool, build with the open-core stack) and **contribute new IP to the IP Pool** (what they create can be valued and added to the commons, with tithing back to the creators).
- **Use the IP:** Builders license and build with the stack the commons holds (holonic architecture, OpenSERV agent stack, Hitchhikers theme as overlay). They form companies, ship products, run ventures — all using the same IP the pool licenses.
- **Contribute IP:** Builder-created IP (agents, ventures, modules, data) can be **contributed or licensed into the IP Pool** on agreed terms (e.g. fair value, capped tithing back to the team). The pool grows with valuable new IP; creators get paid back the same way as OASIS and Hitchhikers contributors.
- **Events:** In-person or online events (e.g. hackathons, 42 locations, July 4 / Burning Man) are **one way** to onboard builders and activate the fund — not the primary definition of the fund. The primary definition is: **use the IP, contribute IP to the pool.**

---

## 7. Fundraising and sign-on

- **Collective fundraising:** The shared plan is intended to be **signed by the participating groups** and used for **collective fundraising** (e.g. family offices, impact capital, grants) for the commons and for agreed initiatives (e.g. Builders Fund, shared roadmap).
- **Separation:** Clear separation between “commons” (stewardship, licensing, payback) and “commercial” (ventures that license from the pool) so investors and partners know what they are backing.
- **Sign-on:** Groups sign up to this plan (or an agreed summary) to signal alignment and to support joint fundraising and go-to-market.

---

## 8. Agreed next steps (from discussion)

1. **Align** on principles: IP in a pool, tithing to pay back creators/contributors, capped return, foundation for research and recognition, Next Gen World unchanged in role and titles, freedom for commercial ventures.
2. **Agree** fair valuation and cap for the tithing return; document in legal and (if desired) technical (blockchain) form.
3. **Engage** trusted legal and structural partners (e.g. same type of IP/stewardship expertise as Hitchhiker's) to draft the pool and licensing structure in stages.
4. **Take the structure to OpenSERV, Hitchhiker's, and LFG** for formal sign-on and use it to secure commitment, funding, and go-to-market.
